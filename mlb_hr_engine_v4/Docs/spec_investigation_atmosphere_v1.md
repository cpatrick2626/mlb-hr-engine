# Investigation Atmosphere Doctrine — MLB HR ENGINE
**Version:** 1.0
**Step:** 9/12 — Tactical Animation, Motion Governance & Investigation Atmosphere
**Status:** Doctrine Only — No Runtime Implementation

---

## 1. Governing Philosophy

Each operational mode in MLB HR ENGINE has a **distinct atmospheric identity**.

Atmosphere is not achieved through visual effects. It is achieved through:
- Density of visible information
- Visual tension calibration
- Pacing of data presentation
- Negative space usage
- Environmental stability

The platform has four primary operational atmospheres: **MAIN**, **JIG**, **FULL SLATE**, and **DEPLOYMENT**. Each demands a different cognitive mode from the operator. The interface must support that cognitive mode — not fight it.

---

## 2. MAIN — Measured Scanning and Qualification

### Operational Identity:
MAIN is the **qualification surface**. The operator is scanning for viable threats, building mental models of the day's slate, filtering signal from noise.

This is the widest aperture in the investigation pipeline. Many players in frame. No single player consuming all attention yet.

### Atmospheric Character:
- **Pace:** Deliberate. No urgency. Broad field of view.
- **Density:** Medium. Enough cards visible to compare. Not so many that comparisons degrade.
- **Tension:** Low-ambient. Escalation signals are present but not demanding.
- **Energy:** Methodical scan. Like a radar operator sweeping, not reacting.

### Environmental Doctrine:

**Visual Density:** Cards display qualification-level information. Full suppression detail not yet exposed. Trust tier visible. Score visible. Narrative summary visible. Deep analytics behind JIG entry.

**Negative Space:** Generous. Cards breathe. Operator is reading across many cards — crowding degrades scan quality.

**Escalation in MAIN:** Escalation badges are visible and count-accurate. CRITICAL badges have persistent border per tier doctrine. But MAIN does not scream — it flags. The operator decides when to investigate.

**Color Tone:** Muted operational palette. No ambient color pressure. Escalation color appears only where it is earned.

**Motion in MAIN:** Minimal. Feed updates occur per feed rules. New escalation signals fire per motion governance. Otherwise: static. The operator is thinking.

**Focus Pacing:** No auto-advance. No popups. No unsolicited expansion. Operator controls aperture entirely.

### What MAIN must never feel like:
- Stock ticker — constant movement, constant distraction
- News feed — content arriving faster than processing capacity
- Leaderboard — competitive, pressured, gamified
- Dashboard wallboard — passive observation, no action surface

### What MAIN must feel like:
The operator is the senior analyst at a morning briefing. Documents on the table. Some flagged. Priorities clear. Time to decide where to dig.

---

## 3. JIG — High-Focus Tactical Investigation

### Operational Identity:
JIG is the **surgical investigation surface**. One player. Full depth. Maximum intelligence density. The operator has selected a threat and is now determining conviction.

This is the narrowest aperture. All other signals recede. One target in full light.

### Atmospheric Character:
- **Pace:** Intensive. The operator is reading deeply. Time slows down.
- **Density:** High within the card. Low outside it. Peripheral noise eliminated.
- **Tension:** Focused. The operator is building or destroying conviction. The data speaks.
- **Energy:** Like a surgeon — no wasted motion, full attention on the field.

### Environmental Doctrine:

**Peripheral Suppression:** When JIG is active, sidebar animations stop. Feed animations pause or reduce to non-motion badge updates only. Nothing competes with the investigation.

**Panel Depth:** JIG surfaces the full intelligence stack — statcast, matchup history, suppression signals, trust score derivation. This depth is earned by the operator's choice to enter JIG. It must be presented without visual clutter.

**Data Hierarchy in JIG:** Most operationally critical signal at top (trust tier, conviction level). Supporting evidence below. Historical context at depth. Operator reads from conviction signal → evidence → context. Never the reverse.

**Visual Tension:** The JIG panel should feel like reading a file. Not a profile. Not a stat sheet. A file. Evidence organized for decision-making.

**Suppression Data in JIG:** When suppression signals are strong, suppression data should feel visually *heavier* than supporting data. Not bigger — weightier. Color saturation slightly higher on suppression indicators than on supporting indicators.

**Negative Space:** Less generous than MAIN — operator is reading depth, not scanning breadth — but no element should feel crowded. Text breathes. Data tables have clear row separation.

**Motion in JIG:** Near zero. Data arrives via skeleton → content crossfade on load. After that: static. The operator is reading. Nothing moves.

### What JIG must never feel like:
- Product page — marketing, not intelligence
- Wikipedia — reference without operational context
- Fantasy stats sheet — raw numbers without interpretive hierarchy
- Slot machine — visual activity while data loads

### What JIG must feel like:
The operator has pulled the file on this player. The room is quiet. The evidence is laid out. The decision is theirs to make.

## 4. FULL SLATE — Battlefield Surveillance and Escalation Management

### Operational Identity:
FULL SLATE is the **command-level surveillance surface**. All active threats visible simultaneously. Escalation state visible across the entire day's field. Priority management and cross-position awareness.

This is maximum aperture. Everything in frame. Operator is commanding, not investigating.

### Atmospheric Character:
- **Pace:** Active. Data changes during the session. Escalation signals accumulate.
- **Density:** High. This is the densest view in the platform. Operator must be able to orient in under 3 seconds.
- **Tension:** Elevated. Not anxiety — operational urgency. The day's bets are in motion.
- **Energy:** Command room. The operator is managing a battlefield, not reading a document.

### Environmental Doctrine:

**Visual Hierarchy:** FULL SLATE must maintain strict visual hierarchy even at maximum density. CRITICAL cards visible above HIGH above MEDIUM above LOW. Not by reordering only — by visual weight. Critical cards carry more visual mass.

**Escalation Management Surface:** FULL SLATE is where the operator **acts on escalation** — not just observes it. Action surfaces (suppress, deploy, escalate-to-JIG) must be reachable from FULL SLATE without requiring JIG entry first.

**Scan Path:** Layout should support a natural scan path. Top-to-bottom priority order. Left-to-right within tier. The operator's eye should never need to zig-zag across the page to understand threat priority.

**Color Pressure:** In FULL SLATE, color coding earns more weight. The operator is managing by visual tier — color is the fastest communication channel at this density. Use it precisely.

**Motion in FULL SLATE:** More active than MAIN or JIG but governed by motion budgets. Escalation events may fire more frequently. Budget rules still apply. Animation queue prevents visual chaos during simultaneous escalations.

**Feed in FULL SLATE:** Feed runs actively. New intelligence items arrive. But feed items must not dominate — they are supplementary to the card grid. Feed may be positioned as secondary column, not primary.

**Compression Behavior:** At high card counts, cards may compress to summary-only display. Summary display must still communicate: player name, trust tier, confidence score, escalation status. Nothing else required at summary density.

### What FULL SLATE must never feel like:
- Fantasy lineup manager — entertainment-forward, low stakes
- Trading terminal — data density without tactical hierarchy
- Social media feed — endless scroll, no priority signal
- Video game HUD — aesthetic complexity masking operational clarity

### What FULL SLATE must feel like:
The operator is standing at a battlefield map. Forces visible. Priority sectors marked. Command decisions required. The map is clear. The next move is obvious.

## 5. DEPLOYMENT — Controlled Conviction and Operational Discipline

### Operational Identity:
DEPLOYMENT is the **commitment surface**. The operator has investigated, decided, and is now acting. This view supports confirmation and execution of wager placement.

This is the highest-stakes operator action in the platform. The atmosphere must match.

### Atmospheric Character:
- **Pace:** Deliberate. Slow is appropriate. The operator should not feel rushed.
- **Density:** Low. One position, or a small portfolio, in clear focus. Distraction eliminated.
- **Tension:** Controlled conviction. Not anxiety — certainty moving toward action.
- **Energy:** Surgical finality. The decision is made. This is execution.

### Environmental Doctrine:

**Signal Suppression During Deployment:** All peripheral escalation signals are deferred during active deployment flow. The operator is committing capital. Nothing should compete for attention.

**Risk Visibility:** Risk summary must be prominent and clear at deployment. Not buried. Not minimized. The operator must see what they are committing before they commit it. This is not a friction mechanism — it is operational discipline.

**Confirmation Hierarchy:** Deploy confirmation must feel weighty. The button communicates finality. Color, weight, and position should all reinforce that this action is consequential.

**Post-Deploy State:** After commitment, the deployment panel transitions to deployed state immediately. No ambiguity about whether the action completed. The deployed position card is static, marked, and clear.

**Undo Doctrine:** If undo is not available, the system must make this clear before deployment. Post-deploy state must never suggest reversibility that doesn't exist.

**Visual Restraint at Deployment:** Deployment is the *least* animated view in the platform. The operator's attention is entirely on confirmation, not on the interface. Motion fires only for the confirmation signal (per deployment animation rules). After that: static.

**Queue Management:** If operator has multiple deployments queued, each is executed sequentially with full confirmation surface. No bulk-deploy-without-review flow.

### What DEPLOYMENT must never feel like:
- Checkout flow — retail, transactional, impersonal
- Casino bet slip — fast, gamified, urgency-manufactured
- Pop-up confirmation dialog — dismissible, low-stakes feel
- Forced urgency — "deploy now before odds change" pressure

### What DEPLOYMENT must feel like:
The operator is signing the order. The intelligence work is done. The conviction is formed. The capital is allocated. This moment is deliberate, controlled, and consequential.

## 6. Atmospheric Transitions Between Modes

Mode transitions are not just navigation events — they are **cognitive mode shifts**. The transition motion should support the shift, not fight it.

| Transition | Duration | Motion | Atmospheric Signal |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------------------|
| MAIN → JIG | 180ms fade | Peripheral content recedes, target expands | Narrowing focus |
| JIG → MAIN | 150ms fade | Target reduces, context returns | Widening aperture |
| MAIN → FULL SLATE | 220ms fade | Single view expands to grid | Scope expansion |
| FULL SLATE → DEPLOYMENT | 200ms fade | Grid recedes, position isolates | Focus and finality |
| Any → DEPLOYMENT | Deliberate, not fast | Nothing rushes to deploy | Operational gravity |

## 7. Lighting Philosophy

"Lighting" in a web interface is expressed through:
- Background contrast
- Panel depth (z-axis visual separation)
- Color saturation on key elements
- Opacity of secondary elements relative to primary

### Doctrine:

**Primary elements are lit. Secondary elements are present.**
In every mode, the operator should be able to identify the primary surface (the card in focus, the decision at hand) in under 1 second. Primary elements have higher contrast, slightly richer saturation, and fuller opacity.

**Depth reads as importance.**
Cards with higher escalation tier appear visually "closer" — higher contrast, sharper borders, fuller opacity. Lower-tier cards recede slightly — lower opacity, lighter borders.

**Background is neutral.**
No texture, no gradient, no ambient color in background. The background is the stage floor — it must not compete with the performers.

**Dark mode is the operational default.**
Light emanating from data surfaces on a dark background mirrors the visual grammar of operations centers, radar rooms, and command displays. This is not aesthetic preference — it is the interface grammar that operators of tactical systems are pre-trained to read.

## 8. Panel Depth Philosophy

Visual depth communicates hierarchy without requiring labels.

### Depth Layers (front to back):

| Layer | Content | Visual Treatment |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 — Foreground | Active modals, deployment surface | Highest contrast, solid background |
| 2 — Primary | Cards in current view, main panels | Full opacity, defined borders |
| 3 — Secondary | Sidebar, feed column | Slightly reduced opacity, lighter borders |
| 4 — Background | Page background, inactive view remnants | Lowest contrast, no borders |

**Rule:** Elements must not appear to float on the same plane when they are in different depth layers. Visual separation must be legible without requiring color alone to communicate it.

**Shadow usage:** Subtle shadows permitted to separate Layer 1 from Layer 2. No decorative shadows. No dramatic drop shadows. Depth, not drama.
